6. Food & Clothing

Food Plan

Monthly Food Budget: $600

Strategies to save on food expenses:

  • Shop at discount grocery stores like Aldi or Walmart for budget-friendly options.
  • Meal planning: Create a weekly menu to avoid impulse purchases and reduce waste.
  • Buy in bulk: Purchase pantry staples like rice, pasta, and canned goods at wholesale stores like Costco or Sam's Club.
  • Avoid pre-packaged meals: Opt for fresh, whole ingredients to save money.
  • Limit dining out: Cook most meals at home and treat dining out as an occasional splurge.

Weekly Menu (7-Day Plan, $150 Budget):

  • Day 1: Breakfast: Oatmeal with fruit ($3.00), Lunch: Turkey sandwich, chips, and a drink ($6.00), Dinner: Grilled chicken, steamed broccoli, and rice ($8.00)
  • Day 2: Breakfast: Smoothie with bananas, spinach, and protein powder ($4.00), Lunch: Leftover grilled chicken with a side salad ($5.00), Dinner: Spaghetti with marinara and garlic bread ($7.00)
  • Day 3: Breakfast: Scrambled eggs, toast, and orange juice ($4.00), Lunch: Peanut butter and jelly sandwich, apple, and chips ($5.00), Dinner: Baked salmon, quinoa, and roasted vegetables ($10.00)
  • Day 4: Breakfast: Yogurt with granola and berries ($4.00), Lunch: Chicken Caesar wrap and a drink ($6.00), Dinner: Tacos with ground beef, cheese, lettuce, and salsa ($9.00)
  • Day 5: Breakfast: Bagel with cream cheese and coffee ($5.00), Lunch: Leftover tacos with a side of chips ($6.00), Dinner: Stir-fried vegetables with tofu and rice ($8.00)
  • Day 6: Breakfast: Pancakes and syrup with orange juice ($5.00), Lunch: Grilled cheese sandwich and tomato soup ($6.00), Dinner: Takeout pizza, two slices ($10.00)
  • Day 7: Breakfast: Cereal with milk and fruit ($4.00), Lunch: Salad with grilled chicken, nuts, and vinaigrette ($8.00), Dinner: Homemade lasagna with garlic bread ($9.00)

Weekly Total: $150

Clothing Plan

Professional Clothing for Health Education Specialist:

  • Business casual attire is standard, including dress shirts, slacks, skirts, blouses, and comfortable dress shoes.
  • Occasional need for formal business attire like suits or blazers for presentations or conferences.

Monthly Clothing Budget:

  • Initial Wardrobe Setup:
    • Dress shirts/blouses: $30 each (3 items = $90)
    • Slacks/skirts: $40 each (2 items = $80)
    • Dress shoes: $60 (1 pair)
    • Accessories: $20
    • Total for initial purchase: $250
  • Ongoing Costs: Budget for occasional replacements or new additions: $50 per month.
