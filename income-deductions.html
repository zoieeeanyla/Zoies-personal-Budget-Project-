2. Income & Deductions

This page focuses on the deductions subtracted from the gross pay to calculate your net pay. Deductions include federal and state taxes, FICA taxes, Medicare, health insurance, life insurance, and retirement contributions. Gross pay is before taxes and Net pay is what you get after.

Employee Benefits at the CDC

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) offers a comprehensive benefits package to its employees, including:

  • Health Insurance: The CDC provides comprehensive medical benefits, including health insurance options. CDC Jobs
  • Life Insurance: Employees have access to life insurance benefits through the Federal Employees' Group Life Insurance (FEGLI) program. CDC Jobs
  • Retirement Savings: The CDC participates in the Federal Employees Retirement System (FERS), which includes:
    • Basic Benefit Plan: A pension plan providing annuity payments upon retirement.
    • Social Security: Contributions to Social Security for additional retirement benefits.
    • Thrift Savings Plan (TSP): A retirement savings and investment plan similar to a 401(k), allowing employees to contribute a portion of their salary. U.S. Office of Personnel Management
  • Employer Match: The CDC offers a generous employer match of 10% if you contribute at least 2% of your earnings after three months of service and after the attainment of age 21. TIAA
  • Additional Benefits:
    • Paid Leave: Includes vacation, sick leave, and other leave programs.
    • Flexible Work Schedules: Options for flexible work arrangements.
    • Employee Assistance Programs: Support services for personal and professional challenges.
    • Wellness and Fitness Programs: Resources to support employee health and well-being.

Sources: CDC Jobs
